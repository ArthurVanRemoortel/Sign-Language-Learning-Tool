Who is this tool for?

This tool is for everyone! From beginners to experiences signers.

Sign languages are also not just for the deaf, but for everyone who wished to communicate with the deaf, such as:

  • Hearing children of deaf parents
  • Hearing siblings and relatives of the deaf
  • Hearing adults who are losing their hearing

VGT Courses

VGT (Vlaamse Gebarentaal) is a sign language used in the Flanders region of Belgium. The "Vlaamse Gebarentaal Centum" is a team of experts and researchers that provides VGT resources and courses. They also created the very helpfull VGT Dictionary. vgtleren.be also offers online courses and learning material.

LSFB Courses

LSFB (Langue des Signes de Belgique Francophone) is a sign language used in the Wallonia region of Belgium. lsfb.be is the go-to resource to find a course in your area.

FAQs

What is a sign language?

Sign languages are languages. They arise naturally through contact between different people, in this case deaf people. Each sign language has its own grammar. In sign language, you speak with your hands and body, listening is done with your eyes. Sign languages are gestural-visual languages, spoken languages are oral-auditory languages.

Is sign language universal?

No. In Spain you have Spanish Sign Language, in Finland you have Finnish and in Wallonia you have Walloon Sign Language. Just because sign languages, like spoken languages, arose spontaneously, there is no one universal sign language. For instance, Dutch Sign Language (NGT) is very different from VGT, and then again VGT shows more similarities with Walloon Sign Language (LSFB) than with Dutch. Sign languages have their own history separate from spoken languages.

How do you sign names?

Usually a sign language alphabet is used to spell the names of individuals.

For people who are part of the Deaf community or who are often the subject of conversation (such as politicians), name gestures exist. Instead of always having to finger-spell a person's name, that person gets one gesture. You get a name gesture based on a typical (appearance) characteristic, a trait, a hobby, a quirk, or simply on the basis of your first name or family name. Cities and countries also often have name gestures.

You usually get your legal name from your parents, a name gesture is actually rarely given by a parent (except perhaps when the parent is Deaf themselves). You also don't choose your name gesture yourself, you get it from other people.
